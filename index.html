How we choose our picks

We do not run our own lab tests. Instead, we rely on the collective wisdom of long‑term owner feedback, published evaluations from clinical journals and reputable consumer outlets, and the track record of each manufacturer. We look for consistency in accuracy across a range of finger sizes and perfusion levels, as well as build quality that withstands daily drops and travel. The most common failure points in pulse oximeters are battery contacts, sensor misalignment, and display fading. Our recommendations avoid models with a history of those issues. We also prioritize devices that are FDA‑cleared, widely available through major retailers, and supported by a responsive warranty or return policy.
