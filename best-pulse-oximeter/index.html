A pulse oximeter clips onto your finger and measures your blood oxygen saturation (SpO2) and pulse rate in seconds. While these devices became common during the pandemic, they remain useful for monitoring respiratory conditions, high-altitude activity, or general wellness. When choosing a pulse oximeter, you want consistent readings from a well-regarded brand, a bright and easy-to-read display (OLED is generally better than LCD in dim light), and a comfortable fit. Some models offer Bluetooth syncing or extra features like plethysmograph waveforms, but for most people a simple, accurate, and durable design is all you need.
